# 📊 Customer Segmentation and Prototype Extraction Report

## 1. Executive Summary

This report outlines the methodology and findings of a two-phase machine learning pipeline applied to our bank's client dataset. The objective was to discover distinct customer groups and translate them into interpretable, deployable profiles for personalized marketing and advisory services.

We achieved this by combining **Unsupervised Learning (Bayesian Gaussian Mixture Model)** to discover natural groupings, followed by **Supervised Learning (Learning Vector Quantization)** to extract definitive "prototypes" for each segment.

---

## Unsupervised Discovery (Bayesian GMM)

To understand the natural structure of our client base, we applied a Bayesian Gaussian Mixture Model (BGM). The algorithm automatically pruned overlapping components and settled on **5 distinct customer personas**. The segmentation is primarily driven by life stage, economic capacity, and behavioral engagement:

- **Persona 0: Mature Mainstream Working Families (51.35%)**
  - _Profile:_ Average age 59, medium family size, medium income/wealth, moderate debt.
  - _Behavior:_ Balanced financial behavior, moderate digital usage.
  - _Dominant Traits:_ Female, Employee/Worker, Nord, Medium-sized city.
- **Persona 1: Urban Middle-Aged Female Accumulators (15.80%)**
  - _Profile:_ Around 53 years old, smaller families, slightly higher income/wealth than Persona 0.
  - _Behavior:_ Higher digital adoption and more active lifestyle.
  - _Dominant Traits:_ Female, mostly Employee/Worker, strongly Nord, Large city.
- **Persona 2: Elderly Male Retirees (Conservative) (8.01%)**
  - _Profile:_ Oldest segment (about 81), small households, low income/wealth, very low debt.
  - _Behavior:_ Low digital engagement, low lifestyle/luxury orientation.
  - _Dominant Traits:_ Male, Retired, Nord, Medium-sized city.
- **Persona 3: Affluent Digital Professionals (15.22%)**
  - _Profile:_ Around 52 years old, highest income/wealth among all clusters.
  - _Behavior:_ Highest financial education, strongest digital adoption, high ESG and lifestyle/luxury orientation.
  - _Dominant Traits:_ Mostly Male, Employee/Worker, strongly Nord, Large city.
- **Persona 4: Elderly Female Retirees (Low-Intensity Banking) (9.63%)**
  - _Profile:_ About 82 years old, low income/wealth, low debt.
  - _Behavior:_ Low digital activity and low lifestyle/luxury propensity.
  - _Dominant Traits:_ Female, Retired, Nord, Medium-sized city.

## Operationalizing Personas with LVQ

While the BGM is excellent for discovery, it is mathematically complex (a "black box") and computationally heavy for real-time predictions. To solve this, we used the 5 persona labels generated by the BGM as the "ground truth" to train a **Generalized Learning Vector Quantization (GLVQ)** model.

- **Methodology:** The LVQ algorithm compressed each complex cluster down into a single representative coordinate known as a **Prototype Vector**.
- **Business Value:** Instead of calculating complex probabilities, the system can now classify a new client simply by measuring their geometric distance to these 5 standard prototypes. The closest prototype dictates the client's persona, making the decision process 100% transparent and highly deployable in production environments.
